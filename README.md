# ✨ Sección 1: Reading & Writing – Fill in the Blanks

## ¿En qué consiste?

Se te presenta un texto académico con **espacios en blanco**.  
Debes elegir la palabra correcta de un menú desplegable (generalmente 4–6 opciones por espacio).

**Habilidades evaluadas:** Gramática, vocabulario, collocations, coherencia textual.

---

## 🔹 Gramática Clave

### 1. Collocations (palabras que van juntas)
Las collocations son combinaciones de palabras que el inglés "prefiere".  
No puedes traducirlas literalmente.

| ✅ Correcto | ❌ Incorrecto |
|-----------|-------------|
| make a decision | do a decision |
| raise concerns | rise concerns |
| caused by | effected by |
| have an impact on | do an impact on |
| carry out research | make research |
| pay attention | give attention |

---

### 2. Prepositional Phrases (expresiones con preposiciones)

| Expresión | Ejemplo |
|-----------|---------|
| responsible **for** | Scientists are responsible for the results. |
| interested **in** | She is interested in biology. |
| result **in** | Pollution results in health problems. |
| result **from** | Health problems result from pollution. |
| caused **by** | The damage was caused by flooding. |
| associated **with** | Stress is associated with poor health. |
| contribute **to** | Exercise contributes to well-being. |
| depend **on** | Success depends on effort. |
| consist **of** | The committee consists of 5 members. |
| focus **on** | The study focuses on urban poverty. |

---

### 3. Word Formation (formación de palabras)

Reconocer si el espacio necesita un **sustantivo, adjetivo, verbo o adverbio** es clave.

| Sustantivo | Verbo | Adjetivo | Adverbio |
|-----------|-------|----------|---------|
| pollution | pollute | polluted / polluting | — |
| development | develop | developed / developing | — |
| significance | signify | significant | significantly |
| reduction | reduce | reduced | — |
| analysis | analyse | analytical | analytically |
| suggestion | suggest | suggestive | — |

---

### 4. Estructura Pasiva (Passive Voice)

Muy común en textos académicos:

| Activa | Pasiva |
|--------|--------|
| Scientists discovered X | X was discovered by scientists |
| The government introduced the law | The law was introduced by the government |
| Human activity causes climate change | Climate change is caused by human activity |

**Clave:** después de `is/was/are/were` → necesitas el participio pasado (caused, reduced, found, shown...)

---

## 🔹 Ejemplo Resuelto

**Texto:**  
*Many scientists believe that climate change is ______ by human activities such as deforestation and pollution.*

| Opciones | ¿Correcto? | Razón |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| A) caused | ✅ | "caused by" = estructura pasiva correcta |
| B) leading | ❌ | "leading" necesita "to", no "by" |
| C) effect | ❌ | "effect" es sustantivo, no verbo |
| D) resulted | ❌ | "resulted" necesita "from" o "in", no "by" |

**Respuesta: A) caused**

## 💡 Estrategias para el Examen

1. **Lee la oración completa** antes de elegir — necesitas el contexto total
2. **Identifica el tipo de palabra** que necesita el espacio (¿sustantivo? ¿verbo? ¿adjetivo?)
3. **Verifica la preposición** que acompaña la opción — muchas veces ahí está la clave
4. **Elimina opciones imposibles** gramaticalmente primero
5. Si hay dos opciones gramaticalmente posibles, elige la que forma una **collocation natural**
6. **No dejes espacios en blanco** — no hay penalización por intentar
